Stucco Repair

Stucco is one of the most robust materials on the market. But due to its rigid nature, stucco can develop cracks and holes over time due to sediment and surface damage.
When a crack or hole appears, it is necessary to take prompt action before any more damage can occur and further penetrate the surface area.

You can try to repair cracks yourself but the money you save in the short term will cost you in the long run if you improperly repair your stucco. The future repairs costs can potentially run into the thousands.

Harbor Stucco employs a team of stucco repair specialists to do the job right. Leave it to us to inspect and repair your property. You'll be glad you did.

Tex-Cote Removal

Tex-Cote is an outer coating that provides structure to the surface to which it is applied. It is sometimes referred to as the ideal choice for keeping a weather-proof house.

Unfortunately, with enough time, you may begin to see peeling, cracking, and flaking. This is usually a symptom of improperly applied Tex-Cote or plain age.

Your Questions Answered

How long does stucco last?

Stucco repair is a very sturdy and beautiful exterior finish, with an average life cycle of 50-80 years or more. Stucco boast's the lowest average repair rates relative to other siding materials. While it's one of the most resilient surfaces available, keep in mind that your stucco job will require a maintenance schedule to keep its fantastic look and stop the risks of developing hairline cracks that might become an issue down the line. Ideally, stucco should be painted every 5 to 10 years to retain its luster.

Can you put new stucco over old stucco?

New stucco may be added over the old one, as long as the necessary steps to ensure the new layer's stability are taken. You'll first need to test your substrate base and exterior finish's structural integrity. Then determine the best approach for the job.
